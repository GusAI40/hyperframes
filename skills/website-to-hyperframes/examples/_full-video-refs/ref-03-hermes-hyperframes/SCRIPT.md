## Open questions for iteration

1. **"Easings" or "Easing curves"?** Single word is tighter and matches the naming-not-explaining principle, but feels clipped. Two words is more readable; it's a 1.8s beat so either fits.
2. **Beat 18 spec readout — drop entirely?** "1920 × 1080. 30 fps." reads like spec-sheet padding. We could let the visual carry it (no VO) and tighten the video to ~39s.
3. **"Your agent makes videos now."** — keep or change? Strong but might land weak after the partnership lockup. Alternatives: "*Hermes makes videos now.*" / "*The skill is yours.*" / "*Now your agent ships video.*"
4. **First line variant:** "Hermes has learned a new skill" vs "Hermes acquires a new capability" vs "Hermes has a new skill." First is closest to V1; others trade warmth for sharpness.
5. **Movement III claim:** "Deterministic. Identical. Every time." is the strongest line. Worth keeping verbatim or paraphrase?
6. **Movement IV order:** Lottie / Captions / Templates is the visual order in the parade. VO follows. Reorder if a different priority matters (e.g. lead with Captions if that's the headline feature).
